Accounts【账户】 & Permissions【权限】

作者： Lian

日期： 2018年05月21日 20:05

An account【账户】 is a human-readable【可阅读】 identifier【身份】 that is stored【存储】 on the blockchain【区块链】. Every transaction【交易】 has its permissions【许可】 evaluated【评估】 under the configured authority【权限】 of an account. Each named permission has a threshold【阈值】 that must be met for a transaction signed【签名】 under that authority to be considered valid【有效】. Transactions are signed【签名】 by utilizing【利用】 a client【客户】 that has a loaded and unlocked wallet【钱包】. A wallet is software【软件】 that protects【保护】 and makes use【使用】 of your keys. These keys may or may not be granted【授权】 permission【许可】 to an account authority on the blockchain【区块链】.

Wallets【钱包】

Wallets are clients【客户】 that store【存储】 keys that may or may not be associated【关联】 with the permissions of one or more accounts【账户】. Ideally【理论上】, a wallet has a locked【锁定】 (encrypted) and unlocked【解锁】 (decrypted) state that is protected by a high entropy password. The EOSIO/eos repository【仓库】 comes bundled with a command line interface【命令行接口】 client called cleos that interfaces with a lite-client called keosd and together, they demonstrate【显示】 this pattern【特征】.

Accounts【账户】

An account is a human-readable【人类可读】 name that is stored on the blockchain. It can be owned by an individual【个体】 or group of individuals depending on permissions【许可】 configuration【配置】. An account is required to transfer【转让】 or otherwise push a transaction【交易】 to the blockchain.

Authorities【权限】 and Permissions【许可】

Authorities determine【决定】 whether or not any given action【行为】 is properly【合理】 authorized【授权】.

Every account has two native【本地】 named permissions

  • owner authority

symbolizes【象征】 ownership【拥有权】 of an account. There are only【只有】 a few transactions that require this authority, but most notably, are actions【行为】 that make any kind of change to the owner authority. Generally【一般】, it is suggested that owner is kept in cold storage【冷存储】 and not shared with anyone. owner can be used to recover【恢复】 another permission that may have been compromised【妥协】.

  • active authority

is used for transferring【转让】 funds, voting【投票】 for producers and making other high-level【高层次】 account changes.

In addition to【除了】 the native【本地】 permissions, an account can possess【处理】 custom【定制】 named permissions that are available to further extend【扩展】 account management. Custom permissions are incredibly flexible【灵活】 and address numerous【很多】 possible use cases when implemented. Much of this is up to the developer community【社区】 in how they are employed, and what conventions if any, are adopted.

Permission for any given authority【授权】 can be assigned【分配】 to one or multiple public keys or a valid【有效】 account name.

Putting【放在】 it all Together【一起】

Below is the combination【组合】 of all the above concepts【概念】 and some loose examples of how they might be practically【实际】 employed.

Default【默认】 Account【账户】 Configuration (Single-Sig)

This is how an account is configured after it has been created【创建】, it has a single【单一】 key for both the owner and active permissions, both keys with a weight【权重】 of 1 and permissions both with a threshold【阈值】 of 1. The default configuration requires a single signature【签名】 to authorize【授权】 a action for the native permissions.

In the @bob account example, this table shows that @bob’s owner key has a permissioned weight of 1, and the required threshold to push【推送】 a transaction under that authority is 1.

To push a transaction under the owner authority, only @bob needs to sign【签名】 the transaction with his owner key for the transaction to be eligible for validation【校验】. This key would be stored in a wallet, and then processed【处理】 using cleos.

Multi-sig Accoun【账户】t & Custom【自定义】 Permissions

The below examples are authorities for a fictional【虚构】 account named @multisig. In this scenario【场景】, two users are authoritized to both the owner and active permissions of a fictional @multisig account, with three users permissioned to a custom publish permission with varying【不同】 weight.

In this scenario【场景】, a weight threshold of 2 is required to make changes to the owner permission level, which means that because all parties have a weight of 1, all users are required to sign the transaction for it to be fully authorized【授权】.

To send【发送】 a transaction【交易】 which requires the active authority, the threshold is set to 1. This implies that only 1 signature is required authorize【授权】 a action from the active authority of the account.

There’s also a third【第三方】 custom named permission called publish【发布】. For the sake【目的】 of this example, the publish permission is used to publish【发布】 posts to the @multisig’s blog using a theoretical【假想】 blog dApp. The publish permission has a threshold【阈值】 of 2, @bob and @stacy both have a weight of 2, and a public key has a weight【权重】 of 1. This implies that both @bob and @stacy can publish without an additional【额外】 signature【签名】, whereas the public key requires an additional signature in order for a action under the public permission to be authorized【授权】.

Thus the above permissions table implies【表明】 that @bob and @stacy, as owners of the account, have elevated【提升】 priviledges similar to a moderator【主持人】 or editor【编辑】. While this primitive【原始】 example has limitations【局限】 particularly with scalability【扩展性】 and is not necessarily a good design, it does adequately【恰当】 demonstrate the flexible nature of the EOSIO permissions system.

Also, notice in the above table, permissions【权限】 are set using both an account【账户】 name and a key【私钥】. At first glance this may seem trivial【无用】, however it does suggest some added dimensions【层面】 of flexibility【灵活】.

Observations【观察者】

@bob and @stacy can be explicitly identified as the owners of this account
The public key cannot push a action under publish authority without an additional signature【签名】 from @bob or @stacy
@bob and @stacy can push a action【行为】 under publish authority without any additional signatures.
